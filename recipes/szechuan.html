Szechuan Chicken

Spicy, Garlic Chicken

Country of Origin : China

Coming from the Szechuan province of China, this dish showcases the Szechuan 'flower pepper', which produces a mild numbingness when consumed.

Ingredients List

  • 1 lb boneless chicken, cut into bite sized pieces
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, eggplants, or snowpeas all work great)
  • 2 bunches scallions, sliced into 1 inch strips
  • 1 ½ tbsp chili paste ( Sambal or chili garlic sauce)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 5 tbsp soy sauce
  • 5 tbsp sherry cooking wine
  • 5 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 4 tsp cornstarch
  • dried red chilis
  • 2 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns
  • Grapeseed or other high temperature oil

Directions

  1. Whisk 3 tbsp soy, 3 tbsp sherry, 2 tsp sugar, and 2 tsp cornstarch. Marinate with chicken at least 30 minutes.
  2. Blend chili paste, garlic, and ginger (minced or grated).
  3. Whisk together remaining soy, sherry, sugar, sesame oil, and vinegar.
  4. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a hot wok on high.
  5. Add chicken (strained) to wok and cook until done, 5 minutes.
  6. Remove chicken, add peppercorns and a handful of red chilis.
  7. Add vegetables for 1 minute, then chicken.
  8. Add chili/garlic mixture, followed by remaining sauces.
  9. Add remaining cornstarch with 2 tbsp water to thicken the sauce as desired.
  10. Stir, remove from heat, and serve.

Notes

Those chilis and peppercorns sure are hot. No need to eat them all, feel free to remove them after cooking for a milder meal.
