Mapo Tofu

Spicy Pork and Tofu

Country of Origin : China

Mapo Tofu is another Sezchuan dish, mixing salty and spicy with the numbness of Szchuan peppercorns.

Ingredients List

  • 1 block (19oz) silken tofu
  • ¼ ground pork
  • 3 tbsp Szechuan spicy bean paste
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp canola or grapeseed oil
  • 3 tbsp chili oil
  • 1 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns, roast and ground
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp fermented black beans, rinsed and pounded
  • 2 stalks scallions, 1-2 inch slices
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • ½ cup water

Directions

  1. Cut the tofu into bitesized cubes.
  2. Heat wok and add oils.
  3. Add pork, garlic, bean paste and stir fry until pork is half cooked.
  4. Add chili powder, soy, black beans and cook until aromatic.
  5. Add tofu and water, stiring gently.
  6. Lower heat and simmer 3-5 minutes or until sauce thickens.
  7. Add peppercorn powder and scallions, mix well.
  8. Serve hot.

Notes

Mapo Tofu literally means pock-marked, old lady tofu due to its appearance. If this dish is too spicy, feel free to reduce chili oil and powder to suit your tastes.
